What security certifications does ChatGPT Business align with?

OpenAI publishes alignment with GDPR, CCPA, CSA STAR, and SOC 2 Type 2 for ChatGPT Business. Alignment with a certification is not the same as your organisation automatically inheriting compliance — you still need to map your own data flows, retention needs, and processor agreements, and confirm which specific controls apply to your workspace tier.

  • CCPA — California Consumer Privacy Act alignment

How does ChatGPT Business change what IT can see and control?

Personal ChatGPT accounts give IT no visibility at all — no usage data, no way to enforce data handling rules, no audit path if something goes wrong. ChatGPT Business moves that work into an organisation-owned workspace with member management and usage visibility, so IT can see who has access and manage the workspace centrally instead of relying on individual accounts nobody can inspect.

What is the actual security risk of doing nothing?

Staff paste client data into consumer ChatGPT accounts with no organisation ownership, no usage visibility, and no audit path — that is the starting position for most UK organisations before any workspace decision is made. Policy bans alone tend to push the same behaviour underground rather than removing it, because the underlying productivity need does not disappear when a policy document says no. The practical risk is not hypothetical: it is client or employee data leaving the organisation's control through accounts nobody in IT can see, with no way to demonstrate what happened if a client or regulator later asks.

A business workspace does not eliminate every risk associated with generative AI use — no vendor certification does that. What it changes is whether your organisation has any visibility or control at all. Moving from zero visibility to an organisation-owned workspace with admin oversight is a step change in defensibility, even before you add specific policies or training on top.
