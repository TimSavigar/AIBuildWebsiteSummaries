How should UK SMEs phase ChatGPT Business spend?

The most common mistake in budgeting for ChatGPT Business is treating it as a single purchase decision rather than a phased rollout. A more realistic budget plan splits spend across three stages: a small pilot cohort in weeks one and two to validate workflows and admin controls, a wider rollout in weeks three to six once pilot metrics show safe usage, and an ongoing renewal and governance-maintenance budget from month two onward.

Pricing this way avoids two common failure modes. The first is over-buying seats before anyone has proven a workflow, which inflates the subscription line without producing adoption. The second is under-budgeting onboarding, so IT absorbs configuration and policy work informally, with no time allocated and no way to show leadership what the rollout actually cost. A phased plan puts a number against each stage — pilot licences, onboarding hours, and expansion seats — so the business case survives scrutiny from finance.
