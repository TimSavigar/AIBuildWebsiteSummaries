## What does each stage of the ChatGPT Business training pathway cover?

Training runs in three stages so nobody is handed connectors and custom GPTs before they have covered data red lines. Fundamentals fit a 90-minute session, role playbooks add one workshop per function, and advanced tools follow once pilot metrics justify connector expansion — not before.

- Stage 1 — Fundamentals: safe prompting, review gates, data red lines (90-minute session)
- Stage 2 — Role playbooks: sales, operations, leadership starter workflows (one workshop per function)
- Stage 3 — Advanced tools: connectors, file analysis, custom GPTs (once pilot metrics justify expansion)

## Who should attend training first?

Pilot users and team leads who will model safe usage, plus the workspace owner. Expand when pilot workflows show consistent review quality — training the whole organisation before the pilot cohort has proven a workflow just means more people learning something that might still change.

## What's the difference between a webinar, a course, and a corporate programme?

A live webinar is a scheduled expert session you register for directly — useful for fundamentals or a specific tool comparison, and a low-commitment way to start before scoping anything larger. A structured course is a self-paced learning path, sometimes combining recorded sessions with assessments, that pilot users can work through on their own schedule rather than waiting for a live slot. A bespoke corporate programme is built around your specific workflows, sector, and rollout stage, delivered by a trainer who has seen your AI use policy and knows which pilot workflows you actually approved.

Most UK organisations use a mix: a webinar or self-paced course for individual fundamentals, then a bespoke session for role playbooks once pilot workflows are settled enough to be worth teaching as a repeatable pattern.

## How do we measure whether training worked?

The honest measure is not attendance or a feedback form — it is whether pilot users stop falling back to personal ChatGPT for the workflows training covered. If people who completed fundamentals training are still pasting client data into a personal account a week later, the training did not land, regardless of how positive the session felt at the time. Pair training completion with the same weekly usage check you would run for the pilot generally, rather than treating training as a one-off event with its own separate success metric.

## Is ChatGPT Business training available online or only in person?

Both. Live webinars and structured courses run online; bespoke corporate programmes can be delivered on-site, virtually, or blended depending on your team's preference. Neither format is inherently better — the right choice depends on whether your team is distributed or co-located, and how hands-on the role playbooks need to be.

## Do we need training if staff already use ChatGPT personally?

Yes — personal experience with consumer ChatGPT does not cover admin controls, data red lines, or the approved workflows specific to your workspace. Training bridges the gap between casual use and governed, repeatable work. Confidence with the consumer product can actually work against you here, since staff may assume the same habits transfer directly to a governed workspace when the data rules are different.

## Can training be customised for our sector?

Yes. Corporate programmes use your workflows, policies, and examples — construction, professional services, operations, and more. Generic training that never references your actual sector tends to produce polite attendance and little behaviour change; sector-specific examples are what make role playbooks stick. A construction team practising on a generic marketing prompt learns very little about how to draft a site report; a finance team practising on a generic sales example learns very little about the review gate their compliance function actually needs.

## Does training include governance?

Every programme covers data red lines, review gates, and escalation paths — training without governance recreates shadow AI risk. Teaching people to use ChatGPT Business well and teaching them where the boundaries are is not two separate programmes; it is one programme done properly, and splitting them tends to produce staff who are confident with the tool but vague on what they should not be doing with it.
